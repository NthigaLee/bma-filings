## Introduction

For insurance and reinsurance companies, **investment income is critical to profitability**. Understanding investment returns and portfolio composition helps analysts evaluate:

- **Company profitability** - Does underwriting cover claims, or do they rely on investment income?
- **Risk management** - Is the company's portfolio diversified or concentrated?
- **Capital efficiency** - How well are shareholders' capital and reserves deployed?
- **Financial stability** - Can the company survive claims spikes without investment income?

The BMA Filings dashboard captures three essential investment metrics for the 30 largest Bermuda insurers:

1. **Investment Return (%)** - Total annual return on invested capital
2. **Investment Yield (%)** - Conservative yield from investment income only
3. **Investments to Assets (%)** - Investment concentration in portfolio

## Investment Fundamentals

### Why Insurance Companies Invest

Insurance companies invest premium income and shareholder capital in:
- **Bonds** (60-70% typical) - Fixed income, lower risk
- **Equities** (20-30% typical) - Growth potential, higher risk
- **Real estate** (5-10% typical) - Diversification
- **Cash equivalents** (5-10% typical) - Liquidity

**The Premium Cycle Model:**
```
Premium Collected → Claims Paid (immediately)
                 → Profit Retained (invested for 5-10 years)
```

Insurance reserves must be invested because claims are paid gradually over time, creating a "float" of available capital.

### Key Concept: The Underwriting Cycle

| Year | Premium Pricing | Loss Ratio | Combined Ratio | Investment Income Needed |
|------|-----------------|-----------|---|---|
| Soft market | Low | High | 110% | YES - need investment income to break even |
| Normal market | Medium | Medium | 97-100% | MODEST - underwriting profitable |
| Hard market | High | Low | 85% | NO - underwriting generates profit |

## Key Investment Metrics

### 1. Investment Return (%)

**Formula:**
```
Investment Return % = (Net Investment Income + Investment Gains/Losses) / Total Investments × 100
```

**Components:**
- **Net Investment Income** - Annual cash received from bonds, dividends, real estate
- **Investment Gains/Losses** - Unrealized and realized gains/losses on portfolio
- **Total Investments** - Full investment portfolio value

**Interpretation:**
- **3-5%** - Conservative, typical bond-focused portfolio
- **5-7%** - Moderate, balanced portfolio with some equities
- **7-10%** - Aggressive, higher equity concentration
- **>10%** - Very aggressive or significant capital gains
- **<0%** - Portfolio losses (market downturn scenario)

**Example - Arch Reinsurance 2024:**
- Net Investment Income: $1,489M
- Investment Gains/Losses: $0M (conservative approach)
- Total Investments: $40,476M
- **Investment Return = (1,489 + 0) / 40,476 × 100 = 3.68%**

### 2. Investment Yield (%)

**Formula:**
```
Investment Yield % = Net Investment Income / Total Investments × 100
```

**Components:**
- **Net Investment Income Only** - excludes capital gains/losses
- **More conservative** than Investment Return

**Interpretation:**
- **2-3%** - Low-risk, fixed-income heavy
- **3-4%** - Balanced bond/equity mix
- **4-5%** - Equity-forward portfolio
- **>5%** - Unusual (may indicate alternative assets or data issues)

**Key Difference from Investment Return:**
- **Yield** ignores capital gains (only looks at cash income)
- **Return** includes capital appreciation (total economic gain)

**Use Cases:**
- **Investors:** Use Yield for stable income expectations
- **Analysts:** Use Return for total portfolio performance
- **Regulators:** May use Yield as "core" investment income

**Example - Arch Reinsurance 2024:**
- **Investment Yield = 1,489 / 40,476 × 100 = 3.68%**
- (Same as Return because company had no gains/losses in 2024)

### 3. Investments to Assets (%)

**Formula:**
```
Investments to Assets % = Total Investments / Total Assets × 100
```

**Interpretation:**
- **30-40%** - Low investment concentration (balanced with receivables, cash, other assets)
- **40-60%** - Typical for insurance companies
- **60-80%** - High investment concentration
- **>80%** - Very aggressive, limited operational flexibility

**What It Means:**
- **Low ratio (30-40%):** Company has substantial reserves, cash, or reinsured exposure
- **High ratio (60-80%):** Company is "all-in" on investments
- **Very high (>80%):** Company vulnerable to market downturns

**Example - Arch Reinsurance 2024:**
- Total Investments: $40,476M
- Total Assets: $62,125M
- **Investments to Assets = 40,476 / 62,125 × 100 = 57.2%**

## How to Interpret Results

### Single Company Analysis

**Scenario: Arch Reinsurance 2024**

| Metric | Value | Interpretation |
|--------|-------|---|
| Investment Return | 3.68% | Conservative, bond-heavy portfolio |
| Investment Yield | 3.68% | No capital gains (stable, predictable income) |
| Investments to Assets | 57.2% | Moderate investment concentration - healthy balance |

**Conclusion:** Arch follows a conservative investment strategy, focusing on steady income rather than capital appreciation.

---

### Comparing Two Companies

**Scenario: Company A vs. Company B (2024)**

| Metric | Company A | Company B | Analysis |
|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Investment Return | 5.2% | 2.8% | A has higher total return (more equities?) |
| Investment Yield | 3.1% | 2.8% | A generates more income; B has capital losses |
| Investments to Assets | 45% | 72% | B concentrated; A diversified with cash reserves |

**Insights:**
- **Company A:** Balanced approach, moderate risk, some capital gains
- **Company B:** Aggressive on investments, may be vulnerable to market drops

**Recommendation:** Company A appears more prudent; Company B should increase cash reserves

### Trend Analysis (Year-over-Year)

**Scenario: Company X - 2023 vs 2024**

| Metric | 2023 | 2024 | Change | Interpretation |
|--------|------|------|--------|---|
| Investment Return | 2.1% | 4.3% | +204 bps | Market recovery, stronger gains |
| Investment Yield | 3.5% | 3.2% | -30 bps | Lower interest rates on new bonds |
| Investments to Assets | 58% | 61% | +3% | Growing investment concentration |

**Analysis:**
1. Market recovered in 2024 (capital gains returned)
2. Yield declined due to lower interest rate environment
3. Company reallocated capital to investments (+3%)

**Recommendation:** Monitor yield trend - may continue declining if rates stay low

## Advanced Analysis

### 1. Profitability Analysis Matrix

**How much does the company NEED investment income to be profitable?**

```
Profitability Dependency = Investment Income / Net Income × 100

Result:
- <25% - Underwriting profitable alone
- 25-50% - Balanced (underwriting + investments)
- 50-75% - Heavily dependent on investments
- >75% - Almost entirely dependent on investments
```

**Example:**
- Company with Net Income $100M
- Investment Income $60M
- Underwriting Profit: $40M
- **Dependency = 60/100 = 60%** - Heavily dependent

### 2. Capital Efficiency Analysis

**Return on Total Capital Deployed:**

```
Capital Return = Net Income / (Shareholder Equity + Reserves) × 100
```

High investment returns may offset lower underwriting returns:
- Company with -5% underwriting result but +4% investment return
- **Overall ROE might still be +5-8%** depending on mix

### 3. Duration & Interest Rate Risk

**Consider the investment term:**
- **If Investment Yield is 2.8% and rates are 3.5%** → Portfolio is "old" bonds, rates rising
- **If Investment Yield is 3.8% and rates are 3.5%** → Portfolio is recently invested, well-positioned

Monitor the gap between:
- **Yield on Portfolio** vs. **Current Market Rates**
- **Widening gap** = Opportunity (could earn more with new investments)
- **Narrowing gap** = Portfolio well-positioned

## Limitations & Considerations

### 1. Data Timing Issues

**Challenge:** Financial statements are published 60-90 days after fiscal year-end
- Investment values may have changed significantly since December 31
- Market conditions in early 2024 different from late 2023

**Impact:** Returns reflect year-end conditions, not current situation

### 2. Accounting Standards

**Challenge:** "Unrealized gains/losses" depend on accounting method
- **Mark-to-market (MtM):** Shows current market values (volatile)
- **Held-to-maturity:** Shows book value (smooth, conservative)
- **Available-for-sale:** Hybrid approach

**Impact:** Two companies with same portfolio may report different returns

### 3. Currency Exposure

**Challenge:** Insurance companies often invest in foreign currencies
- USD portfolio may gain/lose from currency movements
- Not separately reported in this dashboard

**Example:**
- Company invests €10M in European bonds at 1.08 EUR/USD
- EUR strengthens to 1.15 EUR/USD
- Investment "gains" $700K from currency alone

### 4. Catastrophe Year Volatility

**Challenge:** Years with major cat losses may skew investment analysis
- Company may liquidate investments to pay claims
- Investment portfolio shrinks mid-year
- Returns may be artificially low/high

**Recommendation:** Look at 3-5 year averages, not single year

### 5. Credit Quality Not Captured

**Challenge:** Dashboard shows "Total Investments" but not credit quality
- $100M in AAA bonds is very different from $100M in junk bonds
- Credit spread risk not visible

**Recommendation:** Consult company 10-K for portfolio quality breakdown

### 6. Concentration Risk Not Captured

**Challenge:** "Total Investments" may be concentrated
- 80% in single country, single sector, or single credit
- Dashboard only shows total %, not composition

**Recommendation:** Use "Fixed Maturities - AFS" and "Equity Securities" breakdowns

## Using the Dashboard for Analysis

### Quick Analysis (2 minutes)

1. **Select company**
2. **Check Investment Return %** - Is it positive and reasonable (2-6%)?
3. **Check Investments to Assets %** - Is it normal (45-65%)?
4. **Check Industry Average** - How does company compare to peers?

**Decision:** Company is in or out of acceptable range?

### Medium Analysis (10 minutes)

1. **Trend the metrics** - Switch between 2023 and 2024
2. **Identify changes** - What improved/declined?
3. **Calculate dependency** - Does company depend on investment income?
4. **Compare peers** - Select 3-5 competitors, compare metrics

### Deep Analysis (30+ minutes)

1. **Download Excel export** of full metrics
2. **Calculate correlations** - Investment Return vs. Profitability
3. **Year-over-year analysis** - How did markets affect returns?
4. **Peer benchmarking** - Create ranking by metric
5. **Read 10-K** - Understand investment strategy and constraints

## Investment Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Fixed Maturities** | Bonds (corporate, government, municipal) |
| **Available for Sale (AFS)** | Bonds marked-to-market quarterly |
| **Trading Securities** | Bonds actively traded for gain |
| **Equity Securities** | Stocks held by insurance company |
| **Net Investment Income** | Annual cash received (interest, dividends) |
| **Realized Gains/Losses** | Gains/losses from selling securities |
| **Unrealized Gains/Losses** | Mark-to-market gains on securities still held |
| **Float** | Insurance company's unreturned premiums (available to invest) |
| **Duration** | Weighted average time to receive cash from bonds |
| **Spread** | Yield difference between security and benchmark |

## FAQ

**Q: Why is Investment Yield lower than Investment Return?**
A: Returns include capital gains; yield only includes income. Capital gains can be large in up markets.

**Q: Is 3% Investment Return good?**
A: Yes, it's typical for conservative insurance portfolios. Peer average is 3.2%.

**Q: What if Investment Return is negative?**
A: Markets may be down (capital losses). Check Investment Yield - if still positive, company still earning income.

**Q: Why does Investments to Assets % vary so much (15% to 89%)?**
A: Different strategies. Some companies keep cash reserves; others are fully invested.

**Q: How does investment income affect total profitability?**
A: It's critical - often 40-60% of underwriting profit comes from investments.

**Q: Can I predict next year's investment return?**
A: No - depends on market returns, rate changes, and portfolio allocation decisions.

**Q: Why doesn't the dashboard show investment type breakdown?**
A: It does - see "Fixed Maturities," "Equity Securities" in comparison table.
